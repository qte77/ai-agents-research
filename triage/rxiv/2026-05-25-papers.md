## Extracts

### Vector Policy Optimization: Training for Diversity Improves Test-Time Search

- arxiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22817>
- summary: The paper introduces Vector Policy Optimization (VPO), an RL algorithm designed to enhance the diversity of solutions generated by language models in novel environments and inference-scaling search procedures.
- methods: Vector Policy Optimization, reinforcement learning
- findings: VPO matches or beats the strongest scalar RL baselines on test-time search.; The performance gap widens as the search budget grows.; VPO can solve problems that GRPO models cannot.

### AwareVLN: Reasoning with Self-awareness for Vision-Language Navigation

- arxiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22816>
- summary: The AwareVLN framework enhances Vision-and-Language Navigation by integrating a self-aware reasoning mechanism for improved understanding of agent-state and task progress.
- methods: self-aware reasoning mechanism, structural reasoning module, automatic data engine
- findings: AwareVLN significantly outperforms previous state-of-the-art vision-language navigation methods.

### Remember to be Curious: Episodic Context and Persistent Worlds for 3D Exploration

- arxiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22814>
- summary: This work demonstrates that effective curiosity in reinforcement learning requires a persistent model of the world and an episodic trajectory history, enabling agents to explore complex 3D environments more effectively.
- methods: online 3D reconstruction, sequence model over RGB observations
- findings: Agents can become trapped in local loops without spatial persistence and episodic context.; The proposed design enables effective exploration during training.; The agent outperforms RL-based active mapping baselines and generalizes zero-shot to other environments.

### GesVLA: Gesture-Aware Vision-Language-Action Model Embedded Representations

- arxiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22812>
- summary: The study introduces a Gesture-aware Vision-Language-Action model (GesVLA) that enhances robot manipulation by integrating gesture as an instruction modality to resolve spatial ambiguities in complex scenes.
- methods: dual-VLM architecture, two-stage training strategy
- findings: Incorporating gesture improves target grounding accuracy.; Gesture enhances human-robot interaction efficiency in complex environments.

### MOSS: Self-Evolution through Source-Level Rewriting in Autonomous Agent Systems

- arxiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22794>
- summary: MOSS is a self-evolving system that enables source-level adaptation for autonomous agentic systems, improving their performance without human intervention.
- methods: self-rewriting, deterministic multi-stage pipeline, code modification, ephemeral trial workers, user-consent-gated container swap
- findings: MOSS improves a four-task mean grader score from 0.25 to 0.61 in a single cycle; source-level adaptation is Turing-complete and a superset of text-mutable scopes

### LCGuard: Latent Communication Guard for Safe KV Sharing in Multi-Agent Systems

- arxiv: <https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22786>
- summary: The study introduces LCGuard, a framework designed to enhance the safety of KV-based latent communication in multi-agent LLM systems by reducing sensitive information leakage while maintaining task performance.
- methods: representation-level transformations, adversarial training, empirical evaluations
- findings: LCGuard reduces reconstruction-based leakage; LCGuard lowers attack success rates; LCGuard maintains competitive task performance compared to standard KV-sharing baselines
